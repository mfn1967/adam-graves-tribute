Adam Graves

A terror on the ice, a humanitarian off the ice.

Adam Graves NHL Career

  • 1986 - 1987. Drafted 22nd overall by the Detroit Red Wings and led the Windsor Spitfires to the Ontario Hockey Leagure championship.
  • 1987 - 1988. Split time between the Windsor Spitfires (OHL) and the Detroit Red Wings (NHL).
  • 1988 - 1989. Played in 56 games for the Red Wings while splitting time with the Adirondack minor league club, but was only able to score seven goals.
  • 1989 - 1990. Traded from the Detroit Red Wings to the Edmonton Oilers. Graves would go on to score 11 points in the playoffs, en route to defeating the Boston Bruins for the Edmonton Oilers' fifth Stanley Cup.
  • 1991 -1992. Signed an offer sheet with the New York Rangers and scored 26 goals in his first season in New York. Graves finished fifth in the voting for the Frank J. Selke Trophy as the best defensive forward in the league.
  • 1992 - 1993. Although Graves continued to improve in the 1992–93 season, tallying 36 goals and 65 points, the Rangers failed to make the playoffs.
  • 1993 - 1994. Graves scored 52 goals, setting a New York Rangers' franchise record for most goals in a season. In the spring of 1994, Graves helped the Rangers win their first Stanley Cup since the 1939–40 season.
  • 1994 - 2000. Graves remained productive during his tenure with the Rangers, being a consistent 20 to 30 goal scorer until the 1999–2000 campaign. Although the Rangers would not return to the hey-day of their 1993–94 campaign, Graves remained one of the team's most popular players, loved for his strong work ethic and selfless efforts to improve the lives of low income children in New York City.
  • 2001. Graves was traded to the San Jose Sharks in exchange for Mikael Samuelsson and Christian Gosselin.
  • 2004. Adam Graves retires from professional hockey at age 36.
  • 2009. The New York Rangers and The Windsor Spitfires both retire Adam Graves jersey number (#9).
